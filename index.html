Research

I am a researcher specializing in computational seismology, with a diverse focus that includes analyzing large seismological datasets and applying advanced modeling techniques through high-performance computing. My work aims to deepen our understanding of earthquakes, the seismic cycle, crustal structure, and temporal seismic velocity changes for monitoring volcanic and fault zone activity. A significant aspect of my research involves seismic ambient noise interferometry. I investigate temporal seismic velocity changes associated with recurring volcanic eruptions on the Reykjanes Peninsula, Iceland. Additionally, I employ interferometric methods to study co-seismic and post-seismic velocity changes caused by earthquakes in the South Iceland Seismic Zone (SISZ), a region prone to M6–7 earthquake sequences. My work extends to physics-based volumetric seismic cycle simulations spanning several thousand years, providing insights into the earthquake sequences and aseismic slip within the SISZ. These efforts aim to bridge theoretical models and real-world observations, leveraging advancements in exascale computing through the European ChEESE-2P project to enhance seismic hazard assessments in Iceland. Another area of my research focuses on distributed acoustic sensing (DAS), which I use to analyze high-resolution datasets for volcanic tremor detection and monitoring. I am passionate about geophysical fieldwork and have participated in collaborative DAS experiments in Iceland, including DASBummBumm and FagraDASfjall. Additionally, through the Icelandic IS-Noise and IS-Tremor projects, I contributed to several seismic nodal deployments across Iceland, enabling detailed seismic monitoring and data collection in diverse environments. My interests also include full-waveform tomography, a focus of my doctoral research. During this time, I developed a 3D radially anisotrpic subsurface model for western Turkey and the sea of Marmara, capturing previously unknown crustal features beneath major fault zones. Through my research, I aim to contribute to the advancement of earthquake science in Iceland and Turkey, enhancing our capacity to monitor seismic and volcanic events in real-time.
